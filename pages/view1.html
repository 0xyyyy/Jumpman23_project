Delivery Metrics

The following is an analysis of the pickup and dropoff patterns for Jumpman23.

Order Time

In order to grow our userbase and drive repeat business with existing customers we must ensure a smooth user experience. To this end I looked at how long it takes for users to place an order once they've started using the app.






  • Average Time to Order:
    7 minutes 41 seconds


  • Max Time to Order:
    73 minutes


  • % of orders that are outliers:
    7%

Takeaway:

Although average order time is relatively low, it is alarming that such a high percentage of orders lie within the outlier range. This could indicate an issue with the user interface. In order to reduce the amount of idle time spent on the app we could implement a popular restaurants category as well as using information from the users past orders to suggest restaurants.

Prep Time, Transit Time, and Total Order Time

Moving on from User interface concerns, the next area for improvement is order fulfillment time.






  • Average time for Total Order Time, Transit Time, and Prep Time:
    44 minutes, 14 minutes, and 18 minutes


  • Few outliers relative to total data points.
Takeaway:

Average order time is around 45 minutes with a greater portion of this time spent prepping the food. In order to reduce lag between jumpmen arriving at the pickup location and leaving the pickup location, restaurants with high order volume should prep food ahead of time in order to meet demand. To get a better understanding of the types of vehicles Jumpmen are using to fulfill orders I decided to look into popular modes of transportation.

Fleet Analysis

The NYC fleet consists overwhelmingly of bicycles, with cars trailing behind and other modes of transportation being negligible.


According to the distance travelled by vehicle type it appears that the app needs to do a better job differentiating between vehiclee types when assigning Jumpmen to orders.
